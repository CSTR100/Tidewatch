# Demo villain vessel names

Punny names for the dark vessels in the Bering Sea demo — funny in translation, and funny to native speakers too.

## 1. «Авось» (Avos') — Russian

An untranslatable, beloved Russian word meaning roughly "eh, it'll probably work out somehow." It's the national philosophy of unearned optimism — and the perfect name for a boat that turns off its transponder and just hopes. Russians would laugh because *of course* that boat gets caught.

**Theme:** bad luck / recklessness

## 2. 顺手牵鱼号 (Shùnshǒu Qiānyú Hào) — Chinese

"The Grabs-a-Fish-in-Passing." A one-character prank on the real idiom 顺手牵羊 ("lead away a goat in passing," i.e., casual petty theft), with the goat swapped for a fish. Chinese speakers will catch the swap instantly — it's the kind of pun their newspapers make.

**Theme:** thief

## 3. «Баба-Яга» (Baba Yaga) — Russian

The witch of Slavic folklore whose hut famously can't be found unless *it* wants to be found, and turns its back on visitors. In other words: a vessel that only appears on radar when it feels like it. Naming a dark ship after her is exactly the joke a Russian sailor would make.

**Theme:** witch

## Bonus: 浑水摸鱼号 (Húnshuǐ Mōyú Hào) — Chinese

"Fishing in Troubled Waters" — a real idiom for profiting from chaos, whose short form 摸鱼 is also modern slang for slacking off at work. A boat that does both.

**Theme:** profiting from chaos
